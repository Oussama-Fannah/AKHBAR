Halilhodzic intends to invite Ezzalzouli to join the national team in the finals of the African Nations Cup

Vahid Halilhodzic intends to invite the international player Abdessamad Ezzalzouli to be part of the national team in the finals of the African Nations Cup, according to Hesport sources. FC Barcelona club’s international player is set to receive an invitation to be part of the national team for the next games, set to be held in Cameroon starting January 9 to February 6. Vahid Halilhodzic will be inviting the international player soon, as he is having his file at the office, amid his good performance with the FC Barcelona team, ​​as well as the confidence he gained from Xavi Hernandez, the new FC Barcelona coach.

The 19-year-old participated in three games with the first team of FC Barcelona this season and played mainly as a left-back, while he moved as a secondary right-back. Ezzalzouli is the first Moroccan player to join FC Barcelona. Born in Beni Mellal, started as part of FC Barcelona’s reserve this summer from Hercules in Alicante, where he had played 22 games in Division 2B and the Copa del Rey. Previously, Ezzalzouli represented the Moroccan Under-20 team at the 2020 Arab Cup, scoring two goals in five games.

Six Moroccan tennis players suspended for corruption

The International Tennis Integrity Agency (ITIA) announced the provisional suspension of six top Moroccan tennis players this December 14. The cases were adjudicated by an independent anti-corruption hearing officer, Charles Hollander, who found the players guilty of various match-fixing offenses back in July of this year, in addition to charges of receiving money for fixing and failing to report corrupt approaches. The ITIA investigation found that the players’ offenses were linked and therefore were subject to a joint hearing.

Six months later, the ITIA issued the final sanctions against the Moroccan players. Amine Ahouda is banned for 11 years and fined $5,000, Anas Chakrouni, banned for 10 years and fined $5,000, Ayoub Chakrouni is banned for life and fined $10,000. Mohamed Zakaria Khalil is banned for 9 years, fined $5,000, while Soufiane El Mesbahi is suspended for 9 years and fined $5,000, and Yassir Kilani, banned for 9 years, is fined $5,000. The sanctions mean that the players are prohibited from playing in or attending any tennis event authorized or sanctioned by any international tennis governing body or national association for the length of their bans. The players’ suspensions begin from the date of their provisional suspension – 13 July 2021 and they have 20 business days from the date of receipt of the decision to lodge an appeal with the Court of Arbitration for Sport.
